Terminal Pods

High-throughput modular accommodations engineered for airports, railway stations, and bus terminals.

Overview

Precision-Built for Transit Environments

Terminal Pods are compact, efficient pod units designed to maximize guest throughput in high-traffic transit locations. German-engineered for durability, acoustic insulation, and ease of maintenance — delivering a premium rest experience even in the busiest environments.

  • Acoustic insulation for noise-free sleep
  • Biometric lock and digital check-in
  • Integrated climate control
  • High-speed Wi-Fi and USB charging
  • Premium mattress and bedding
  • Shared or private bathroom options

Applications

Where Terminal Pods Excel

International Airports

Perfect for layovers, flight delays, and early departures. Position within or adjacent to departure terminals for maximum convenience.

Railway Stations

Serve overnight rail travellers and waiting passengers at major railway junctions and long-distance train hubs.

Bus Terminals

Intercity bus terminals with overnight or multi-hour connections benefit significantly from compact pod accommodations.

FAQ

Common Questions

What is the minimum booking duration? +
Terminal pods can be booked for a minimum of 4 hours, making them ideal for short layovers and transit rest. Full overnight bookings (up to 24 hours) are also available.
How is hygiene maintained between guests? +
Each pod undergoes a full sanitization protocol after every checkout, including UV-C light treatment, professional linen replacement, and surface disinfection compliant with international hospitality standards.
What is the investment required to deploy terminal pods? +
Terminal pod deployments begin from ₹60L for a minimum cluster of 8 pods. Investment scales with pod count. Contact our franchise team for a site-specific proposal.
Can terminal pods be expanded after initial deployment? +
Yes. The modular design allows incremental expansion. Additional pod units can be deployed alongside existing installations without disrupting operations, typically within 4–6 weeks per expansion phase.
